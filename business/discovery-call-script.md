# Discovery Call Script & Guide

**B&B Global Services LLC** — *From Idea to Operations*

Use this guide to run a structured, consultative discovery call. The goal is not to pitch — it's to understand the prospect's situation, qualify fit, and recommend the right next step (usually a focused assessment such as the Technology Health Check).

**Target call length:** 30–45 minutes.

---

## Pre-Call Prep Checklist

- [ ] Review the prospect's website, LinkedIn, and recent news.
- [ ] Identify likely industry (Healthcare, Financial Services, SaaS & Technology, Professional Services, Real Estate & Property Management, Hospitality, SMB, PE Portfolio Company).
- [ ] Note approximate company size (ideal fit: 25–5,000 employees).
- [ ] Form a hypothesis about which service pillar(s) likely apply.
- [ ] Confirm who is attending and their roles (decision-maker vs. influencer).
- [ ] Have the engagement offers and pricing ranges handy.
- [ ] Prepare the booking link: https://calendly.com/bnbglobal/consultation
- [ ] Set up note-taking / CRM record.

## 1. Opening & Rapport (3–5 min)

> "Hi [NAME], thanks for taking the time today. I'm [YOUR_NAME] with B&B Global Services. Before we dive in — how's your week going?"

- Build genuine rapport; reference something specific from your research.
- Briefly introduce B&B in one sentence:

> "Quick context on us: B&B helps companies design, build, secure, deploy, operate, and support technology — basically taking things from idea all the way to reliable operations. We work with companies that have outgrown basic IT support but aren't looking for a giant consulting firm."

---

## 2. Agenda-Setting (2 min)

> "Here's what I'd suggest for today: I'd love to learn about where you are today and what's prompting this conversation, share a bit about how we work where it's relevant, and if it makes sense, we'll talk about a clear next step. Does that work? And is there anything specific you want to make sure we cover?"

- Confirm timing: "We've got [30/45] minutes — does that still work on your end?"

---

## 3. Discovery Questions by Theme

Ask open questions and listen. Let them talk 70% of the time.

### A. Current State
- "Tell me about your current technology setup and how your team is structured around it."
- "Who handles technology today — internal team, an MSP, a mix?"
- "What systems or platforms are most critical to running the business?"
- "How do you handle software delivery / cloud / security / support today?"

### B. Pain Points
*(Listen for these common signals and map them to a pillar.)*

| What you hear | Likely fit |
|---------------|-----------|
| "Our releases are slow / manual deployments" | Cloud, DevOps & Platform Engineering |
| "We have an idea/product but no team to build it" | Application & Product Development |
| "We're worried about security / compliance" | DevSecOps & Cybersecurity |
| "We have outages / no monitoring / no recovery plan" | Reliability, Operations & BCDR |
| "Onboarding, devices, and support are chaotic" | IMAC & Managed Technology Services |
| "We don't have a tech roadmap or leadership" | Strategy & Advisory |
| "No clear ownership after we launch something" | Managed Services / Operate phase |

- "What's not working the way you'd like right now?"
- "What happens today when something breaks?"
- "Where do things tend to fall through the cracks?"

### C. Goals & Vision
- "If we're talking a year from now and this went great, what's different?"
- "What business outcomes are driving this — growth, cost, risk, speed?"
- "Are there any deadlines, launches, or board commitments tied to this?"

### D. Budget & Timeline
- "Have you set aside a budget range for this, or are we still scoping that?"
- "What's your ideal timeline — is this urgent, or planning ahead?"
- "Have you invested in something like this before? How did it go?"

### E. Decision Process
- "Besides you, who else would be involved in a decision like this?"
- "What does your evaluation or approval process usually look like?"
- "Is there anything that would need to be true for you to move forward?"

## 4. Qualifying Criteria

Strong fit when most of the following are true:

- [ ] Company size roughly 25–5,000 employees.
- [ ] Has outgrown basic IT support but not ready for a large consulting firm.
- [ ] A real, named pain point that maps to one of our six pillars.
- [ ] A business driver (growth, modernization, security, reliability, support).
- [ ] An identifiable decision-maker and a rough budget or willingness to scope one.
- [ ] A timeline within the next 1–6 months.
- [ ] Industry alignment (especially Healthcare, Financial Services, SaaS, Professional Services, PE portfolio companies).

If it's not a fit, be honest and helpful — refer them elsewhere if you can. It protects the brand.

## 6. Handling Common Objections

**"You seem expensive / what does this cost?"**
> "Fair question. Our engagements scale to the problem. A lot of clients start small — a Technology Health Check runs $5,000–$15,000 and gives you a prioritized 90-day roadmap. You'll know exactly what's worth investing in before committing to anything bigger."

**"We already have an IT provider / internal team."**
> "That's common, and we often complement existing teams rather than replace them. Where we tend to add value is the gap between basic support and serious delivery — building products, modernizing the cloud, embedding security, or hardening reliability. Where do you feel your current setup is stretched?"

**"We're not ready / the timing isn't right."**
> "Totally understand. Often the lowest-risk first step is just getting clarity — a health check or assessment you can act on whenever you're ready. Would it help to have that roadmap in hand now so you're prepared when timing improves?"

**"How do I know you can deliver?"**
> "Reasonable. We can walk you through relevant case studies — observability transformations, cloud and CI/CD modernization, DevSecOps, BCDR programs, managed support setups. And the assessment itself is a low-commitment way to see how we work before a larger engagement."

**"Why not a big consulting firm?"**
> "If you need a 50-person team for a multi-year transformation, they're a fine choice. Most growing companies don't — they need senior people who'll both advise and actually build and operate, without the overhead and complexity. That's exactly the space we focus on."

## 7. Next-Step CTAs

Always close with a specific, concrete next step. Match it to where they are:

- **Strong fit, clear pain →** *"Let's run a **Technology Health Check** ($5,000–$15,000): a current-state review, risk analysis, executive summary, and a 90-day roadmap. I can send a short scope this week."*
- **Building a product →** *"Sounds like an **Idea to App MVP** ($25,000–$75,000) — discovery, UX/UI, architecture, MVP build, deployment, and basic support. Want me to outline what that looks like for you?"*
- **Needs ongoing support →** *"We offer **Managed Technology Support** ($1,500–$10,000/month) — user and device support, IMAC, vendor coordination, monthly reporting, and basic security and backup oversight."*
- **Not ready to scope →** *"Let's book a follow-up consultation to go deeper."* → https://calendly.com/bnbglobal/consultation

**Confirm the commitment before ending:**
> "Great — so the next step is [SPECIFIC ACTION]. I'll [SEND SCOPE / EMAIL SUMMARY / BOOK THE FOLLOW-UP] by [DATE]. Does [DATE/TIME] work for the next conversation?"
