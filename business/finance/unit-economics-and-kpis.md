# B&B Global Services LLC — Unit Economics & KPIs

**Purpose:** Define the small set of numbers that actually drive the value of a technology services / managed-services firm, set conservative target ranges, and give the founder a weekly/monthly dashboard to run the business by.

A services firm lives or dies on four things: **how efficiently it acquires clients, how profitably it delivers, how much revenue recurs, and how well it retains clients.** Everything below ladders up to those four.

---

## 1. Why Recurring Revenue & Margin Multiply Valuation

Before the metrics, the "why." Two firms with identical revenue can be worth very different amounts:

- **Recurring revenue is predictable revenue.** A buyer (or a banker valuing the firm) discounts one-time project revenue heavily because it has to be re-won every year. Recurring contracts (Managed IT, Application Maintenance, BCDR retainers, Fractional Advisory) are assumed to continue, so they are valued on a forward, multiple-of-ARR or higher-EBITDA-multiple basis.
- **Margin is the fuel.** Higher gross and EBITDA margins mean more cash to reinvest and a larger profit base to apply a multiple to. A firm at 40% EBITDA is worth far more than the same revenue at 12%.
- **The interaction compounds.** High recurring % + high margin + low churn = the profile acquirers compete for. That is the entire strategic point of land-and-expand: convert one-time clients into durable, high-margin recurring relationships. See `sellability-and-valuation.md` for the multiples.

> **Rule of thumb:** A dollar of high-retention recurring revenue is typically worth several times a dollar of one-time project revenue at exit. Growing the recurring base is the highest-leverage thing the founder can do.

## 2. Core Unit-Economics Metrics

All target ranges are **illustrative** for an early-stage mid-market technology services firm. Replace with your own once you have 6–12 months of real data.

| Metric | Definition | Why it matters | Illustrative target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost) | Total sales & marketing spend ÷ new clients won in the period | Tells you what it costs to land a client; the denominator of all efficiency | **< $5,000** per recurring client early on |
| **LTV** (Lifetime Value) | Avg monthly gross profit per client × expected lifetime (months) | The total profit a client generates over the relationship | **$80,000–$200,000+** for a recurring client |
| **LTV:CAC** | LTV ÷ CAC | The single best efficiency ratio for a recurring business | **≥ 4:1** (3:1 acceptable; <3:1 means fix sales or pricing) |
| **CAC Payback** | CAC ÷ monthly gross profit per client | How fast acquisition cost is recovered | **< 12 months** |
| **Gross Margin** | (Revenue − direct delivery cost) ÷ revenue | Profitability of delivery before overhead | **50–60%** blended; recurring lines 55–65% |
| **Project Margin** | (Project price − contractor/sub & direct cost) ÷ project price | Whether one-time work is actually profitable after sub-labor | **45–55%** per project |
| **Utilization / Billable Rate** | Billable hours ÷ available hours (per person/contractor) | The engine of services profitability; bench time destroys margin | **70–80%** for delivery staff |
| **Effective Bill Rate** | Project revenue ÷ billable hours delivered | Pricing power and scope discipline | Track trend; should rise over time |

## 3. Recurring-Revenue Metrics (the value drivers)

| Metric | Definition | Why it matters | Illustrative target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRR** | Monthly Recurring Revenue — sum of all monthly contract value | The compounding base of enterprise value | Grow every month |
| **ARR** | Annual Recurring Revenue = MRR × 12 | The headline number acquirers and lenders anchor on | $250k (Y1) → $1.8M+ (Y3) run-rate |
| **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** | (Starting MRR + expansion − contraction − churn) ÷ starting MRR | Whether the existing book grows by itself via expansion | **≥ 100%**; **110%+** is excellent |
| **Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)** | (Starting MRR − contraction − churn) ÷ starting MRR | Pure stickiness, ignoring upsell | **≥ 90%** |
| **Logo Churn** | Recurring clients lost ÷ recurring clients at start of period | Early warning on delivery quality and fit | **< 10%** annually |
| **Recurring % of Revenue** | Recurring revenue ÷ total revenue | The mix shift that re-rates the whole firm's value | **30%+ by Y2, 40%+ by Y3** |

> **Net Revenue Retention above 100%** means the existing client base grows revenue on its own — clients expand (add Managed IT, then BCDR, then Fractional Advisory) faster than others churn. This is the clearest sign land-and-expand is working.

## 4. Pipeline & Sales-Health Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Why it matters | Illustrative target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pipeline Coverage** | Total open pipeline value ÷ revenue target for the period | Whether you have enough at-bats to hit the number | **3x–4x** of the target |
| **Win Rate** | Deals won ÷ qualified deals | Sales effectiveness and qualification quality | **25–40%** of qualified opportunities |
| **Average Deal Size** | Total bookings ÷ number of deals | Drives revenue per sales effort; should rise as you move upmarket | Assessments ~$12k; projects ~$85k (illustrative) |
| **Assessment→Project Conversion** | Projects won from assessment clients ÷ assessments delivered | The first land-and-expand gate | **≥ 40%** |
| **Project→Recurring Conversion** | Project clients who buy recurring ÷ project clients | The second, value-creating gate | **≥ 30%** |
| **Sales Cycle Length** | Avg days from qualified lead to signed contract | Cash-flow and forecasting input | Track and shorten |

---

## 5. Risk & Concentration Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Why it matters | Illustrative target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Revenue Concentration** | Revenue from largest client ÷ total revenue | Concentration is the #1 thing acquirers and lenders penalize | **No client > 20%** of revenue (long-term) |
| **Top-3 Concentration** | Revenue from 3 largest clients ÷ total revenue | Broader dependence check | **< 40%** |
| **Founder Dependence** | % of revenue/delivery that requires the founder personally | Directly caps sale value (see valuation guide) | Reduce toward **< 30%** over 1–3 years |
| **DSO** (Days Sales Outstanding) | (Accounts receivable ÷ revenue) × days in period | Cash health; how long clients take to pay | **< 45 days** |
| **Contracted Revenue %** | Revenue under signed MSA/contract ÷ total | Durability and defensibility of the book | Maximize; aim most recurring under MSA |

## 6. The Founder's KPI Dashboard

A short, honest dashboard beats a 40-metric spreadsheet nobody reviews. Track these on a fixed cadence.

### Weekly (15 minutes)

| Metric | Watch for |
|---|---|
| New MRR added / lost this week | Any churn — investigate immediately |
| Pipeline coverage (open pipeline ÷ quarter target) | Falling below 3x |
| Cash position & upcoming AR/AP | Sub payments due before client pays |
| Active deals in late stage | Stalls or slipped close dates |
| Utilization of delivery staff/contractors | Bench time (unbilled capacity) |

### Monthly (60–90 minutes)

| Metric | Watch for |
|---|---|
| MRR, ARR run-rate, and net new MRR | Growth slowing |
| Net & gross revenue retention | NRR dropping below 100% |
| Gross margin and project margin (per project) | Margin erosion / underpriced deals |
| EBITDA and EBITDA margin | Overhead outpacing revenue |
| CAC, LTV:CAC, CAC payback | Acquisition getting inefficient |
| Win rate and average deal size | Qualification or pricing problems |
| Revenue concentration (top client, top 3) | Single-client dependence rising |
| DSO / aged receivables | Slow payers building up |
| Assessment→project and project→recurring conversion | The land-and-expand engine stalling |

### Quarterly / Annual

| Metric | Watch for |
|---|---|
| Recurring % of total revenue (mix shift) | Not trending toward 40%+ |
| Founder-dependence % | Not declining |
| Contracted (MSA) revenue % | Too much revenue uncontracted |
| Logo churn (annualized) | Drifting above 10% |

## 7. Quick Reference — Target Scorecard

| KPI | Healthy target (illustrative) |
|---|---|
| Gross margin | 50–60% |
| EBITDA margin | 20–40% |
| Utilization | 70–80% |
| LTV:CAC | ≥ 4:1 |
| CAC payback | < 12 months |
| Net revenue retention | ≥ 100% (110%+ great) |
| Gross revenue retention | ≥ 90% |
| Logo churn | < 10% / year |
| Win rate | 25–40% |
| Pipeline coverage | 3x–4x |
| Recurring % of revenue | 30%+ (Y2) → 40%+ (Y3) |
| Largest-client concentration | < 20% |
| DSO | < 45 days |

> If you only track three numbers, track **net new MRR, gross margin, and net revenue retention.** Those three predict both the health of the business today and its value at exit.
