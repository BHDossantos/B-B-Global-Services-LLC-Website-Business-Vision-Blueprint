# Go-to-Market Brief

**B&B Global Services LLC** — *From Idea to Operations*

A focused statement of how B&B wins business in its first growth phase. The guiding principle: **keep a broad vision, but go to market narrow.** A founder-led firm cannot credibly be elite at all six pillars at once, so we lead with the sharpest edge of the spear and expand from there.

---

## 1. The Positioning Wedge

**"You've outgrown basic IT, but you're not ready for a big consulting firm."**

This is the gap B&B owns. Below us sit break-fix MSPs and generalist IT shops that keep the lights on but cannot architect, build, or modernize. Above us sit the large consultancies — expensive, slow, partner-led, and overscoped for a 25–500-person company. In between is a growing business with a real engineering or reliability problem and no one senior enough to both advise *and* build *and* operate it.

B&B is that one trusted partner: enterprise-grade practice, hands-on delivery, no enterprise overhead. We don't just advise — we build, operate, secure, and recover.

---

## 2. The Lead Pillar — Tip of the Spear

Of the six pillars, our go-to-market lead is:

> **Cloud, DevOps & Platform Engineering  +  Reliability, Operations & BCDR**

**Why this wedge:**

- **It's where the founder's experience is deepest.** Our strongest case studies are observability transformation, cloud delivery modernization, CI/CD + DevSecOps implementation, data platform reliability, and a tested BCDR program. We lead with proof, not promises.
- **The pain is acute, expensive, and board-visible.** Outages, manual deployments, failed releases, and untested recovery plans are quantifiable risks executives already feel. They convert.
- **It is recurring by nature.** Reliability and BCDR work naturally graduates into SRE retainers, monitoring ownership, and recovery-testing cadence — recurring revenue, not one-off projects.
- **It is defensible for a small firm.** Senior reliability and platform engineering is genuinely scarce in the mid-market; we are not competing against commodity labor.

The other four pillars — Strategy & Advisory, Application & Product Development, DevSecOps & Cybersecurity, and IMAC & Managed Technology Services — remain in the catalog and the vision. We sell them when a client pulls us into them, and we fulfill the overflow through contractors and partners (see Section 5) until revenue justifies hiring.

## 3. The Assessment-Led Funnel (Land and Expand)

Every relationship is designed to start small, prove value, and grow. The **Technology Health Check ($5,000–$15,000)** is the low-risk front door.

```
Health Check  ->  Roadmap  ->  Implementation  ->  Managed Services / Retainer
  (land)         (the plan)     (the build)        (expand + recur)
```

| Stage | Offer | Typical value | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Land** | Technology Health Check | $5,000–$15,000 | Low-risk entry. Current-state review, risk analysis, 90-day roadmap. Earns trust and surfaces the real scope. |
| **Plan** | Roadmap / Resilience or Cloud Assessment | $7,500–$35,000 | Converts findings into a prioritized, budgeted implementation plan. |
| **Build** | Cloud/CI-CD, BCDR, or SRE implementation | $15,000–$300,000+ | The core delivery engagement where most revenue is earned. |
| **Expand** | Managed Support, BCDR retainer, Fractional CTO | $1,500–$25,000/month | Recurring revenue and durable ownership after launch — "no hand-off into a vacuum." |

The Health Check is deliberately priced as a *yes* — small enough to approve without a committee, structured enough to make the next step obvious. It is the single most important asset in the funnel.

## 4. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Firmographics**

| Attribute | Target |
|---|---|
| Employee count | 25–500 (sweet spot 50–300); the broader vision serves up to 5,000 |
| Stage | Growing past first scale; legacy-modernizing; post-funding; PE-owned |
| Geography | New Hampshire and Greater Boston first; remote-friendly nationally |
| Tech footprint | Real cloud/app/data systems that are business-critical and under strain |
| Budget signal | Able to approve a $5K–$15K assessment without a procurement cycle |

**Priority industries** (per our positioning): SaaS & Technology, Financial Services, Healthcare, Professional Services — these feel reliability, security, and delivery pain most acutely. Secondary: Real Estate & Property Management, Hospitality, and Private Equity portfolio companies (a strong repeat-and-referral channel — one relationship can seed several).

**Buying triggers to watch for:** recurring outages or incidents, a stalled or risky release process, a cloud bill or migration that's out of control, an upcoming audit or compliance push, an office move or rapid headcount growth, a product idea with no team to build it, or a PE thesis requiring tech due diligence and value creation.

**Ideal buyer:** a founder, CEO, COO, VP Engineering, or Head of IT who feels the pain personally and can authorize a five-figure first step.

## 5. What We Defer to Partners

To stay credible and protect margin, we fulfill non-core demand through a vetted contractor and partner bench rather than overpromising in-house depth:

- **Deep application/product builds** beyond MVP scale — augment with trusted senior engineers.
- **Specialized cybersecurity** (pen testing, formal audit, SOC/compliance certification) — partner with licensed specialists; B&B leads readiness and remediation.
- **High-volume IMAC / service desk** — staff through managed-services partners; B&B owns the model and the client relationship.
- **Net-new strategy/advisory** that isn't anchored to a delivery engagement — taken selectively.

The principle: **B&B always owns the client relationship, the architecture, and the quality bar.** Partners deliver capacity, not the relationship.

---

## 6. Primary KPIs

Three numbers tell us whether the go-to-market is working:

1. **Assessment conversion rate** — % of qualified discovery calls that convert into a paid Health Check or assessment. *This is the engine of the whole funnel.* Target: 30%+.
2. **Land-to-expand rate** — % of assessments that convert into an implementation or recurring engagement within 90 days. *This is where the firm becomes profitable and durable.* Target: 50%+.
3. **Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)** — total from managed support, BCDR, and fractional CTO retainers. *This is the stability metric that justifies hiring and de-risks the founder-led model.*

Supporting metrics to monitor: qualified leads per month, average contract value, and gross margin per engagement type.
