# Outreach Email Sequence — B&B Global Services LLC

A 4-email sequence for reaching former colleagues and business owners, offering a **free 30-minute technology review** (the door-opener to our paid **Technology Health Check**).

**Tone:** warm, brief, value-first. Never salesy. Lead with the relationship and a genuinely useful offer.

**Sending guidance**
- Personalize every `[PLACEHOLDER]` — generic outreach gets ignored.
- Space emails ~3–4 business days apart.
- Stop the sequence the moment someone replies, and move to a real conversation.
- Plain text, no images, no tracking pixels. Send from a personal address that shows as "Bruno at B&B Global Services."

**Signature block (reuse on every email):**

> Bruno Dos Santos
> B&B Global Services LLC — From Idea to Operations
> hello@bnbglobal.net · +1 (603) 555-0142
> New Hampshire & Boston · https://bnbglobal.net

## Email 1 — The Reconnect (Day 0)

**Subject line options:**
- `Quick hello, [FIRST_NAME] — and a bit of news`
- `[FIRST_NAME] — been a while`
- `Reconnecting + something I just launched`

---

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

It's been a while since [SHARED_CONTEXT — e.g. "we worked together at [COMPANY]" / "we last caught up"]. I hope things are going well at [THEIR_COMPANY].

Quick reason I'm reaching out: I just launched **B&B Global Services**, a technology firm for growing companies. We take teams from idea to operations — strategy, software, cloud, security, reliability, and managed support — without the overhead of a big consulting firm.

No ask today. I mostly wanted to reconnect and let you know what I'm building.

If technology is ever a topic on your side, I'd genuinely enjoy comparing notes.

Talk soon,
Bruno

---

## Email 2 — The Value Offer (Day 4)

**Subject line options:**
- `A free 30 minutes on [THEIR_COMPANY]'s tech?`
- `Offer: quick technology review, no strings`
- `[FIRST_NAME] — happy to be a second set of eyes`

---

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

Following up with something concrete.

I'm offering a **free 30-minute technology review** to a handful of people in my network. It's a quick, candid look at where [THEIR_COMPANY] might be carrying hidden risk or friction — things like:

- Slow or risky software releases
- Cloud costs or complexity creeping up
- Security gaps (the "we'll get to it later" kind)
- No tested backup or recovery plan
- Unclear ownership after something ships

You'll walk away with 2–3 specific, useful observations whether or not we ever work together.

If that's helpful, I can share a couple of times this week or next. What works better for you — a call or a quick virtual coffee?

Best,
Bruno

---

## Email 3 — The Proof / Reframe (Day 8)

**Subject line options:**
- `Most companies don't have a tech problem`
- `The pattern I keep seeing, [FIRST_NAME]`
- `One thought before I get out of your inbox`

---

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

One idea that might resonate:

**Most companies don't have a technology problem — they have a delivery problem.**

The tools are fine. What stalls is the delivery: strategy that never becomes a working system, security bolted on too late, vendors pointing at each other, and no clear owner after launch.

That gap is exactly what we close. The same partner advises, builds, secures, deploys, and operates — so nothing falls between the seams.

The free 30-minute review I mentioned is the easiest way to see if any of that is showing up at [THEIR_COMPANY]. If it's useful, it can roll into a deeper **Technology Health Check** — a current-state review, risk analysis, and a prioritized 90-day roadmap. If not, no harm done.

Want me to send a couple of times?

Bruno

---

## Email 4 — The Soft Close (Day 12)

**Subject line options:**
- `Should I close this out, [FIRST_NAME]?`
- `Last note — door stays open`
- `Bad timing? No problem`

---

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

I don't want to crowd your inbox, so this is my last note on it.

If a quick technology review would be useful for [THEIR_COMPANY], just reply with a day that works and I'll send a time. If now isn't the moment, totally understand — the offer stays open whenever it's relevant.

Either way, it's good to be reconnected, and I'd love to stay in touch.

Wishing you and the team well,
Bruno
