# Sales Playbook

**B&B Global Services LLC** — *From Idea to Operations*

The operating manual for moving a prospect from first contact to a recurring relationship. It pairs with the **Discovery Call Script** (how to run the conversation) and the **Proposal Template** (how to document the engagement). This document covers *the system*: stages, qualification, what to pitch, how to handle objections, and the math that ties activity to revenue.

Guiding principle: **lead narrow, expand broad.** We open on our strongest wedge — Cloud/DevOps/SRE and Reliability/BCDR — anchored by the Technology Health Check, then expand into the other pillars as trust compounds.

---

## 1. The Pipeline

Six stages, each with a clear exit criterion. A deal does not advance until its exit criterion is met.

| # | Stage | Goal | Exit criterion |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Lead** | Identify a fit prospect and book a call | Discovery call scheduled |
| 2 | **Discovery** | Understand pain, qualify, recommend a next step | Prospect agrees to a paid assessment or scoped proposal |
| 3 | **Assessment** | Deliver the Health Check / assessment; surface real scope | Roadmap delivered; implementation opportunity identified |
| 4 | **Proposal** | Document scope, deliverables, investment | Proposal sent and reviewed |
| 5 | **Close** | Align, handle objections, sign | SOW/MSA signed; kickoff scheduled |
| 6 | **Expand** | Convert delivery into recurring / cross-pillar work | Retainer or follow-on engagement signed |

The **Assessment stage is the hinge of the entire pipeline.** Because it's paid, it pre-qualifies the buyer, funds discovery, and makes the implementation proposal a natural continuation rather than a cold pitch.

## 2. Qualification Framework — MEDDIC, Adapted

For anything above a Health Check, qualify with MEDDIC. For the Health Check itself, a lighter BANT-style check (see the Discovery Script's qualifying criteria) is enough — the assessment is the qualifier.

| Letter | What we're confirming | Good signal |
|---|---|---|
| **M — Metrics** | The quantified pain or outcome | "Outages cost us X"; "releases take Y weeks"; "no tested recovery plan" |
| **E — Economic buyer** | Who can authorize the spend | Founder/CEO/COO/VP Eng/Head of IT is engaged, not just an influencer |
| **D — Decision criteria** | What they'll judge us on | Senior talent, speed, both advise-and-build, proven case studies |
| **D — Decision process** | Steps and timeline to a yes | Knows approval path; timeline within 1–6 months |
| **I — Identify pain** | A named pain mapped to a pillar | Maps cleanly to Cloud/DevOps, Reliability/BCDR (lead), or another pillar |
| **C — Champion** | An internal advocate | Someone who feels the pain personally and will sell internally |

**Disqualify or refer out** when there's no economic buyer, no budget willingness even for a $5K assessment, no business-critical pain, or the need is pure commodity break-fix. Honest referrals protect the brand.

## 3. The Productized Offers — and When to Pitch Each

Three core offers anchor the catalog. Match the offer to the signal you heard in discovery.

| Offer | Price | Pitch when you hear… |
|---|---|---|
| **Technology Health Check** | $5,000–$15,000 | "Things feel fragile," "we're not sure what to fix first," any strong-fit prospect not ready to commit to a build. *Default first step for almost everyone.* |
| **Idea to App MVP** | $25,000–$75,000 | "We have an idea/product but no team to build it." |
| **Managed Technology Support** | $1,500–$10,000/month | "Onboarding, devices, and support are chaotic," "we need ongoing help." |

Beyond the three core offers, lead-pillar engagements (from the rate card) are pitched directly when discovery surfaces a specific, urgent problem:

- **Resilience / Cloud Assessment** ($7,500–$35,000) — outages, no monitoring, runaway cloud spend.
- **CI/CD or Cloud Modernization** ($15,000–$300,000+) — manual deployments, slow/risky releases.
- **BCDR Program** ($25,000–$150,000) — no tested recovery, undefined RTO/RPO, audit pressure.

When in doubt, **lead with the Health Check.** It's the lowest-risk yes and it surfaces the larger scope for you.

## 4. Objection Handling

Keep these consultative, not defensive. (The Discovery Script has conversational versions; these are the strategic anchors.)

**"You seem expensive / what does this cost?"**
> Reframe to risk and value. "Most clients start with a Health Check at $5,000–$15,000 and get a prioritized 90-day roadmap. You'll know exactly what's worth investing in before committing to anything bigger." Anchor against the cost of an outage or a stalled release, not against an hourly rate.

**"Why not just hire someone internally?"**
> "A single senior reliability or platform hire runs $180K–$250K all-in, takes months to find, and gives you one perspective. We bring a team's worth of experience immediately, you only pay for what you need, and we build the systems and documentation so your future hire inherits something solid instead of starting from zero."

**"Why not a big consulting firm (Accenture / Deloitte)?"**
> "If you need a 50-person team for a multi-year transformation, they're a fine choice — and you'll pay partner rates for junior delivery. Most growing companies need senior people who will both advise and actually build and operate, fast, without the overhead. That's exactly the space we focus on. You'll work with the people doing the work."

**"How do I trust a newer/smaller firm?"**
> Lead with proof and de-risk the first step. "Fair — so let's keep the first step small and concrete. The Health Check is a low-commitment way to see exactly how we work before any larger engagement. And I can walk you through directly relevant case studies: observability transformation, cloud and CI/CD modernization, DevSecOps, and a tested BCDR program." Senior, hands-on access *is* the advantage of a smaller firm — sell it.

## 5. Cross-Sell and Up-Sell Paths

Every engagement is a doorway into the next. Map the natural adjacencies:

| Starting point | Natural next pillar(s) | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Health Check (any)** | Whatever the roadmap prioritizes | The assessment *is* the cross-sell engine |
| **Cloud / CI-CD (lead)** | DevSecOps; Reliability/BCDR | "Now secure the pipeline"; "now make it observable and recoverable" |
| **Reliability / BCDR (lead)** | SRE retainer; Managed Support | "Who owns monitoring and recovery testing ongoing?" |
| **Idea to App MVP** | Cloud/DevOps; App maintenance retainer | "Now deploy it reliably and keep it running" |
| **Managed Support** | Strategy/Advisory; Fractional CTO | "You need a roadmap and technology leadership" |
| **Any project** | Managed Services / retainer | "No hand-off into a vacuum" — own the operate phase |

The highest-value motion is **project → recurring retainer.** Always propose the operate/recover phase as part of, or immediately following, the build.

## 6. Referral and Partner Sourcing

A founder-led firm grows fastest on trust-based channels, not cold volume:

- **Past colleagues and clients** — the warmest source; the case-study relationships are referral seeds.
- **Private equity firms** — one relationship can seed multiple portfolio-company engagements (due diligence, modernization, value creation). High-leverage channel; prioritize it.
- **Partner bench** — the contractors and specialists we fulfill overflow with also refer work back; make it reciprocal.
- **Local ecosystem** — New Hampshire and Greater Boston tech, founder, and professional-services networks; targeted LinkedIn presence and a steady cadence of Insights content.
- **Complementary providers** — MSPs, dev shops, and accounting/legal firms whose clients hit problems above their depth.

Ask every satisfied client for one introduction at the close of a successful engagement. Make referral requests specific ("who do you know with the same release/reliability problem?").

## 7. Funnel Math — Worked Example

Use this to translate a revenue goal into required activity. Numbers are illustrative; replace with your real conversion rates as data accumulates.

**Assumptions (conservative starting estimates):**

| Metric | Assumption |
|---|---|
| Discovery call → paid assessment | 30% |
| Assessment → implementation proposal | 80% |
| Proposal → close | 50% |
| Avg. assessment value | $10,000 |
| Avg. implementation value (lead pillar) | $60,000 |
| Land-to-expand: closes that add a retainer | 50% @ ~$4,000/mo |

**Goal: $500,000 in year-one booked revenue.**

Walking the funnel for one closed implementation client:

```
Implementation closes needed:  ~7  (7 x $60K = $420K)
  + assessment revenue:        ~17 assessments x $10K = $170K
  + retainers:                 ~3-4 clients x $4K/mo  (annualizing through the year)
  -> blended bookings comfortably clear $500K
```

Working *backwards* to the activity that produces ~7 implementation closes:

| Stage | Conversion | Volume needed |
|---|---|---|
| Implementation closes | — | ~7 |
| Proposals sent | 50% close | ~14 |
| Paid assessments | 80% → proposal | ~17 |
| Qualified discovery calls | 30% → assessment | ~57 |
| Leads (calls booked from outreach/referral) | assume 50% of leads take a call | ~115 |

**Operating cadence implied:** roughly **10 quality leads and 5 discovery calls per month** sustains this funnel. That is achievable for a founder-led firm leaning on referrals, PE relationships, and the lead-pillar wedge — and it shows why **assessment conversion** and **land-to-expand** are the two ratios to defend above all others.

## 8. Sales Hygiene

- Log every deal at its current stage with its exit criterion in the CRM.
- Never advance a stage on hope — only on the exit criterion being met.
- Send a recap with the agreed next step within 24 hours of every call.
- Review pipeline weekly against the three primary KPIs (assessment conversion, land-to-expand, MRR).
- Refer out non-fits promptly and graciously.
